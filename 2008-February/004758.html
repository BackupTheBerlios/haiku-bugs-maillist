 When having a long-running process such as a configure script or a
 compilation it is sometimes necessary to review previous output while
 still running (e.g. to evaluate whether to kill it).

 On Linux, the GNOME Terminal behaves like this: When you haven't scrolled
 it assures that the last line is visible (at the bottom, once the screen
 is full). When you have scrolled up it doesn't jump to any new lines
 written until you scroll down to the last line again.

 While the BeOS Terminal doesn't have this feature, it would be handy to
 have it on Haiku. Maybe optional?
