 Well if you are sure that it is r32039 that introduced that problem then
 just say so, to avoid further guessing.

 The problem with BMessage unflattening is that the function doesn't get a
 length argument. It therefore doesn't have any clue how large the handed
 in buffer actually is. That makes returning an error in that case
 impossible, as you can't actually figure out that you're already reading
 behind the buffers end.

 As I haven't seen anything in that direction up to now it's hard for me to
 investigate. The settings file in question or another reproducible test
 case is really the least I'd need.
