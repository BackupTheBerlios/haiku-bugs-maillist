 Well, it's a controller built into the processor, so it has the advantage
 of having less overhead compared to an external controller. It probably
 has a higher precision too, though I wouldn't know the numbers. The local
 APIC is part of processors since the Pentium Pro, so it's nothing just
 recently introduced. The problem is, as mentioned above, that it is not
 strictly necessary on non-SMP systems, so its exposure is far less which
 makes unrecognized bugs more likely.
