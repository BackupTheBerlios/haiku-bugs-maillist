Comment(by axeld):

 It wasn't just null.h, though. You can use \{\{\{/\}\}\} to have correct
 newlines :-)
 In any case, I usually do it this way, and that's also what you'll find in
 95% our sources:
 {{{
 #ifndef NULL
 #       define NULL 0
 #endif
 }}}

 The header guards follow at least one rule: local/(very) private headers
 use NAME_H, public or shared system headers use at least _NAME_H (as the
 underscore is generally a reserved namespace for the operating system, not
 just Haiku). If it's _NAME_H_ or _NAME_H I don't really care, I would just
 follow what the majority of the other POSIX headers does.
