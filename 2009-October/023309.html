Comment(by jprostko):

 I had to recently correct somebody using "Haiku OS" on their website.  My
 advice was to change it to "Haiku", and I went on explaining why.
 Basically, it comes down to the fact that you don't go around saying
 "Windows OS" or "Linux OS", but you would be fine saying "BeOS",
 "ReactOS", or "SkyOS" due to those being the actual names.  I think using
 "operating system" is fine if both words begin lower case, and the phrase
 is used in the course of a sentence, but not if both words begin
 capitalized or the phrase "Haiku operating system" is used standalone.

 So yeah, here's how I see it...

 Invalid forms:

 HaikuOS
 Haiku OS
 Haiku Operating System   <-- or variants like the one atop our home page
 Haiku operating system   <-- when used standalone

 Valid forms:

 Haiku
 Haiku operating system   <-- when used in the course of a sentence
