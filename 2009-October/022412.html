 When you hold the Wacom pen in one position, you'll notice that the mouse
 cursor erratically moves a couple of pixels around one spot. In BeOS,
 Ubuntu and Windows this movement is a lot less, if not non-existent.

 I noticed that the Linux Wacom driver uses a simple moving average filter
 to provide noise correction, so I hacked-up [attachment:wacom_filter.patch
 a patch] that implements this in the Haiku driver.

 Unfortunately this filter doesn't fix the erratic movements completely, so
 I decided to compare the 'raw' tablet data:
  - x-movement (BeOS on the left, Haiku on the right):
  [[Image(x-BeOS.png, 300px)]][[Image(x-Haiku.png, 300px)]]
  - y-movement (BeOS on the left, Haiku on the right):
  [[Image(y-BeOS.png, 300px)]][[Image(y-Haiku.png, 300px)]]
 The horizontal gridlines represent the pixel-boundaries on my
 monitor.[[BR]]
 You can see that the 'raw' movement is a lot bigger in Haiku than in BeOS,
 but I have no idea where the cause of this difference lies.

 Also note the difference in the time between two samples, between BeOS
 (left) and Haiku (right):
  [[Image(dt-BeOS.png, 250px)]][[Image(dt-Haiku.png, 250px)]]
 Although this could have been caused by the different methods used to
 capture the data: to a file in BeOS, to the serial port in Haiku.
