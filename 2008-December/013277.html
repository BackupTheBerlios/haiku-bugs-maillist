 So the data that were written to the disk don't get corrupt. Only the new
 data that are only now being written to disk get corrupt.

 On a BFS partition, when you have say 30 files, each file 20 MiB, do a
 copy of them, wait a few seconds after Tracker has finished copying and
 press F12 and type "reb<cr>". After reboot, you can see the just copied
 files, although, they are corrupt. I suppose that BFS has got journaling
 and the newly copied files after a crash should be either present and not
 corrupt, or not exist at all.
