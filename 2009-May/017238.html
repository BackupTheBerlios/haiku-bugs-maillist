Comment(by jonas.kirilla):

 Various systems do it differently. (I think local time in the hardware
 clock is an old PC thing, whereas GMT is the unix/server way.)

 Being able to specify which method is in use is needed when alternating
 between multiple different operating systems on the same hardware.

 I believe some systems maintain filesystem timestamps in universal time
 and offset the presentation instead. This might be good when traveling.
 Imagine compiling when crossing over to a new timezone, and suddenly some
 of your timestamps went back or forward one hour!
