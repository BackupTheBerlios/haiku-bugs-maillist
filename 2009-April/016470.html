Comment(by humdinger):

 Hmmm, does this really warrant an option?
 I wouldn't mind buttons ignoring clicks within the double-click intervall.
 If you need more than one instance of the app running, which is, let's be
 honest, not a very common use case IMO, calm youself and wait half a
 second between clicks. :)
 In case of the Terminal you could also just open one, then switch to the
 keyboard, as you'll do anyway, and do a few ALT+N.

 Watching the common user, my old father for instance, he apparently doesnt
 get the difference between a button in Windows' quickstart bar and an icon
 on the desktop. He always doubleclicks and ends up with two e.g.
 Thunderbirds (or more, as it takes so long to launch these beasts hat he
 thinks his first few double-clicks were ignored...).

 All I'm saying, is this really worth an option? I think it's better to
 have one default behaviour and stick with it. I'd prefere the ignoring-
 within-doubleclick-interval, but wouldn't be disappointed if you go the
 other way.
