 This is r30071.

 One thing I like of the Gnome thing: when you rename a file, the filename
 is selected without the .suffix. I know Haiku doesn't rely on the suffix
 to determine the filetype, nevertheless we'll have suffixed files for a
 very long time to come. Let's face it, an AVI is an .avi and a MP3 is a
 .mp3...

 Therefore I suggest:[[BR]]
 * ALT+E should enter rename mode without selecting the .suffix[[BR]]
 * Pressing ALT+E again while in rename mode, will select the whole
 filename.[[BR]]
 The detection of the second ALT+E should be real quick, so you can
 basically just double-ALT+E to get there very quickly.
