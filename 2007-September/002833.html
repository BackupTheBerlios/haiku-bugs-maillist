Comment (by marcusoverhagen):

 The commercial devices available that support USB debug port
 specification are rather limited, there seems to be only one
 company that offers a product:

 http://www.ajaystech.com/net20dc.htm
 http://www.ajaystech.com/
 http://developer.intel.com/technology/usb/download/DebugDeviceSpec_R090.pdf
 http://www.intel.com/technology/magazine/computing/it09021.pdf

 Quite a number of laptops offer serial ports on a docking station,
 this can be used, too.

 It's also possible to build parallel-printer-port or ps2-port to RS232
 debugging cables, and use those for debugging. If such a thing is
 required,
 I can offer helping with that.

 Early support of standard USB-RS232 cables isn't feasible.
