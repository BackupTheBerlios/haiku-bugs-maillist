 Haiku drops a message about mouse down in this situation:
 Press alt+f and enter. Now during several seconds tracker will try to find
 all files on partition.
 After that press alt+a to select them all and drag them.
 While roaster try to identify file types (i think this is why this delay
 in 10 seconds) click in anything (e.g. tab bar). This should mean that you
 ended dragging files, but several seconds later you will see that
 transparent selection will stick to your cursor, like you didn't click on
 tab seconds ago.
 BTW, If you press reset while dragging you will corrupt bfs. At least this
 will say serial output along with ide dma errors which will delay booting
 by 30 seconds.
 Should this be documented in another bug? I think i remember similar
 problems in other bug or two.
 Tested in vmware with r22147 under fedora 6.
