Comment:

 Wouldn't it be possible to have gcc4 compiled version of translators in
 /boot/beos/system/add-ons/gcc4 and makes gcc4 bins use them from it.

 I suggest putting GCC4 add-ons on a subdirectory, instead of GCC2 ones,
 because for R5 compatibility, legacy apps expect to find add-ons on
 /boot/beos/system/add-ons/, so legacy add-ons (legacy means GCC2 compiled)
 should be in /boot/beos/system/add-ons/, and new GCC4 add-ons should then
 be relocated in a "gcc4" subdirectory.

 This would allows to use GCC2 apps that make use of add-ons on a GCC4
 compiled Haiku with GCC2 libs (hybrid build).

 Is that a realistic suggestion??
