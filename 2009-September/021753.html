Comment(by stippi):

 Very interesting. When the pen is not in proximation, the wacom tablet
 will never send an interrupt transfer that the driver scheduled. Instead,
 the driver will hit a timeout and reschedule the next transfer (with the
 same timeout). This was designed such that the transfer thread would exit
 cleanly and not block forever when the tablet is unplugged (this was
 necessary on BeOS R5 when the driver was written). Maybe there is
 something wrong with how the driver cleans up (respectively doesn't clean
 up) the timed out transfers.
