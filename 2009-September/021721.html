 When I unplug my Wacom Graphire 2 tablet, a lot of USB errors are showing
 up in the serial log, like:
 {{{
 usb error uhci 0: td (0x0243e4c0) error: status: 0x254507ff; token:
 0x00e88769;
 }}}

 The strange thing is that the amount of errors (after the `wacom:
 interrupt transfer - failure: -2147442668` line) is related to the
 duration the tablet was connected to the computer:
  - 4 lines when the tablet was connected for [attachment:2seconds.txt 2
 seconds].
  - 11 lines when the tablet was connected for [attachment:5seconds.txt 5
 seconds].
  - 20 lines when the tablet was connected for [attachment:10seconds.txt 10
 seconds].

 But when the pen is always in proximity of the tablet when connected to
 the computer, the amount of errors is:
  - 0 lines when the tablet was connected for [attachment:30seconds-
 penontablet.txt 30 seconds].
 Compare that to the amount of errors when the pen wasn't in proximity:
  - 62 lines when the tablet was connected for [attachment:30seconds-
 nopenontablet.txt 30 seconds].

 Because the computer is logging to the serial port, this temporarily
 results in an unresponsive system when disconnecting the tablet. When
 serial debugging is disabled, the system stays responsive but the CPU
 usage is 100% for several seconds (especially when the tablet has been
 connected to the computer for several hours).

 Tested in Haiku R1/alpha1 on real hardware (PII at 400MHz, 384 MB, Intel
 440BX).
