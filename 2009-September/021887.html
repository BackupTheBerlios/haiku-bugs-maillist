Comment(by rogueeve):

 Obviously setting the rate to 0 as mentioned in the book is probably a
 sign of a really-not-very-nice screensaver, but I don't think reasonable
 rates other than that are necessarily cpu hogs-- you also have to take
 into account how much work is being done per tick. In my case I was
 porting a simple screensaver from Linux which does an animation every 10
 milliseconds. Running flat out; I got >2000fps out of this thing, so that
 gives you an idea how little work is being done per frame at the usual
 frame rate.

 I applied the patch I suggested to my own build and it does work.
