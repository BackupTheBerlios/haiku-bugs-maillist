#1970: VM's sAvailableMemory counter is possibly inaccurate
---------------------------+------------------------------------------------
 Reporter:  anevilyak      |       Owner:  axeld         
     Type:  bug            |      Status:  new           
 Priority:  normal         |   Milestone:  R1/alpha1     
Component:  System/Kernel  |     Version:  R1 development
 Platform:  All            |  
---------------------------+------------------------------------------------
 As exposed by the new way of doing get_system_info(), the number given by
 sAvailableMemory appears to be incorrect. After doing something involving
 heavy amounts of I/O / memory allocations, (i.e. a large svn checkout or
 attempting a Haiku build from within Haiku), this number steadily grows
 until it's significantly larger than the amount of physical RAM in the
 system. As an example, after attempting the build, sAvailableMemory
 reports back 4.8GB on a system that has only 1GB of physical RAM. Even
 accounting for overcommits, this seems far too high, especially given lack
 of swap.
