Comment (by axeld):

 I'm not sure why the size is that important. We actually have different
 sizes per family, too, it's just that we stayed compatible with BeOS with
 regards to sockaddr and sockaddr_in. For sockaddr_in6 and others, there is
 no need to stay compatible, though.
 We also have a sockaddr_storage which is 256 bytes in size.

 The last bytes in sockaddr_in are always padding, though, and can be
 neglected.
