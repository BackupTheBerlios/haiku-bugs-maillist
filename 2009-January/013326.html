Comment(by humdinger):

 Maybe you should turn this into a thread on the mailing list for
 discussion.

 I'm not sure losing the "by attribute" search is the right way to go.
 Searching specific attributes is a key feature of our queries. Also, I'm
 not sure limiting searches to date and size is a common usage pattern of
 the average user.
 IMHO, a user is often looking for this music track, that picture or text
 file or some video etc. So, access to these catgories should be as quick
 as possible. Probably by default best without regard to the exact filetype
 (JPEG, GIF, PNG) but the supertype (image/*).

 My totally undeveloped suggestion is to have
 1) Live result window: as soon as you enter information, the result window
 (integrated in the find panel) shows the results. Often the list of hits
 shortens quickly enough so you don't have to come up with more specific
 search criteria.

 2) A filter widget above every displayed attribute column of the results.
 This is where your idea with date and size comes in. By e.g. in/decreasing
 the spinbox of the size column, you filter out the bigger/smaller files
 (needs an additional radiobutton-setting: bigger/smaller, before/after,
 is/not). The same goes for date. Or picture dimension. Or Artist, with a
 filter-textbox.
